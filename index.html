Te Pā Tūwatawata

A six-module kaupapa Māori course on whakapapa o te raraunga, AI, tikanga, and sovereign digital futures. Free to fork, adapt, and translate.

Written for iwi, hapū, whānau, kaiako, and anyone thinking seriously about who owns data, who owns models, and what a decolonised digital architecture actually looks like.

Ngā wāhanga · The Modules

  1. 1. Whakapapa o te raraunga
    The whakapapa of data — where does data come from, and who does it belong to?
  2. 2. Te Pā Tūwatawata hei tauira
    Te Pā Tūwatawata as a model for Indigenous-led digital infrastructure.
  3. 3. AI me te raupatu matihiko
    AI and digital extraction — tracing the colonial logic inside machine learning.
  4. 4. Tikanga, ture, mana whakahaere
    Tikanga, law, and governance — how Māori legal frameworks challenge tech power.
  5. 5. Hoahoa tika
    Designing ethical systems — what does a decolonised AI architecture look like?
  6. 6. He anamata rangatira
    Sovereign digital futures — building Māori-controlled infrastructure for generations.
